# Benjamin's Tears — Story

> A surrealist action-adventure game. The world itself isn't comprehensible to most who live in it.
> There are as many beasts and non-humans as there are humans.
> Most of the story takes place in a city called **X**.

---

## Acts

### I — The Fall

Benjamin falls from a high-rise building. The building itself is very barebones.
During the fall, outlines of angels begin to appear. Benjamin avoids them.
Once through the clouds, he launches straight into the earth.

**Black screen. 10 seconds.**

> *The fall is his death. We started the game with him dying — meaning he's died right before we began playing. This gives the world an omniscient feel. Benjamin is his own entity, and we are merely accompanying him in his conquest to fully understand where he is and where he came from.*

---

### II — Homecoming

Benjamin slowly emerges from the crater. First: sounds. Then: we see him.

He brushes the dust from his suit as if the fall were a mere inconvenience.
Though his appearance is nowhere near godlike. A hybrid between **Franz Kafka** and **Mister Bean**.
Skinny. Almost attractive — though not quite.

---

### III — Renovations

Benjamin's home is an absolute mess. The most recent **Überfall** has left it in a dire state.

An Überfall is a significant event that brings an unavoidable horde of beasts overrunning the city.
Nothing can fully withstand an Überfall; therefore Benjamin must be prepared:
he can purchase equipment and fortification to keep himself safe.

Most importantly — should he fail to keep the place safe, all the documents he's compiled of the world,
his own attempt at understanding where he is and where he came from, will be lost.

---

### IV — Überfall

The monsters are at the gates. They're running toward the town.
Everything is closed: shops, homes. No one is outside. Benjamin rushes inside.

While inside his home he is free to do whatever he wants. He can't go outside.
He can continue trying to understand the world. His stringboard consists of
characters, dialogues, pictures of places, monsters, apparitions.

His willingness to venture into the unknown increases the data he's gathered
on this mysterious world. But so far it doesn't tell us much.

The Überfall seems to prevent the progress we make on understanding the world.
It's about a world that **doesn't want to be understood**. It acts more as a prison.

> *Is it a prison? Are we free to stay? To leave?*

---

### V — A Boy Meets Girl

A beautiful, young redhead — seen skipping around the abandoned church — catches Benjamin's attention.
She is very young but dresses maturely, her very long and poorly adjusted scarf trailing behind her.

**Character introduced: Yumaya.**

## World Notes

- **City X** — the primary setting. Strange, prison-like, resistant to comprehension.
- **Überfall** — recurring event. A horde of beasts overtakes the city. Unavoidable. Preparation is key.
- **The Stringboard** — Benjamin's tool for making sense of the world. Characters, places, dialogues, creatures.
- **The Wall** — a giant vertical plane at the edge of town with a locked door at its center.
- **The Key** — unknown. The central MacGuffin of the second half.
